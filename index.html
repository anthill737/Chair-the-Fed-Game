Chair the Federal Reserve

A Monetary Policy Simulation

Your Role

You are the newly appointed Chair of the Federal Reserve, the central bank of the United States. Your primary tool is the federal funds rate — the interest rate at which banks lend money to each other overnight. By raising or lowering this rate, you influence borrowing costs across the entire economy.

Inflation

Inflation measures how quickly prices rise across the economy. The Fed's target is 2% per year. Too much inflation erodes purchasing power and destabilizes planning. Too little (or deflation) can slow growth and make debt harder to repay.

Higher interest rates tend to reduce inflation by making borrowing more expensive and cooling demand.

Unemployment

The unemployment rate measures the share of workers actively seeking jobs. A healthy economy sustains a natural rate near 5% — some job turnover is normal. Much higher signals a weak economy; much lower can fuel wage-driven inflation.

Lower interest rates tend to reduce unemployment by making investment and hiring cheaper.

The Tradeoff

Monetary policy involves a fundamental tension. Raising rates fights inflation but risks slowing the economy and raising unemployment. Lowering rates stimulates hiring but can push inflation higher. Holding rates steady lets prior policy continue to work — but the economy doesn't always cooperate.

Real-world events — oil shocks, financial crises, productivity booms — will force you to react quickly and sometimes accept imperfect outcomes.

How to Play

  • The simulation runs for 16 quarters (4 years).
  • Each quarter, review current economic conditions and news.
  • Choose to raise, lower, or hold the federal funds rate.
  • Rate changes take effect with a lag — policy doesn't work overnight.
  • Your goal: keep inflation near 2% and unemployment near 5%.
  • At the end, you'll be evaluated and either reappointed or removed.

Scoring

You are scored each quarter based on how close inflation and unemployment are to their targets. Your final score (0–100) determines your reappointment outcome.
